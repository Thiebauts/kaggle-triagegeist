\begin{center}
\begin{minipage}{0.85\textwidth}
\small\textit{
A machine learning system for 5-level Emergency Severity Index prediction,
validated on 418,100 real emergency department visits from the Beth Israel
Deaconess Medical Center. This report describes the discovery of label leakage
in a synthetic competition dataset, the pivot to real clinical data, and the
development of an asymmetric safety framework calibrated to the clinical reality
that under-triage is an order of magnitude more dangerous than over-triage.
The system achieves Cohen's kappa of 0.701, matching the reliability of
live-patient triage by human nurses reported in the Mirhaghi et al. (2015)
meta-analysis of 40,579 cases.
}
\end{minipage}
\end{center}
